Methane Emissions in Australia: Trends, Causes, and Effects

Visual Trends:
The bar graph shows a general increase in methane emissions from 1990 to 2018. Emissions start at a lower level and increase significantly, reflecting the country's rapid economic and industrial growth.

Reasons for Increasing Emissions:

  • Agricultural Activities: Methane is a significant byproduct of livestock digestion and manure management.
  • Energy Production: Extraction and processing of fossil fuels contribute to methane emissions.
  • Waste Management: Decomposition of organic waste in landfills produces methane.
  • Biomass Burning: Burning wood, bushfires, and biofuels release methane.
  • Rice Farming: Methane is produced during rice cultivation in flooded fields.

After Effects:

  • Climate Change: Methane is a potent greenhouse gas, contributing significantly to global warming.
  • Air Quality: Methane emissions can lead to the formation of harmful ground-level ozone.
  • Environmental Impact: Climate changes disrupt ecosystems and biodiversity.
  • Economic Costs: Mitigating climate change impacts can be financially burdensome.
  • Public Health: Methane and associated pollutants can cause respiratory and cardiovascular issues.
